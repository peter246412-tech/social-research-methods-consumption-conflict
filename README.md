# Social Research Methods Project

Research design project for a social science methodology course.

## Topic

**The effect of overseas conflict on essential-goods consumption in South Korean households: A comparative research design on U.S.-Iran conflict periods before and after the rise of hyper-connected society.**

## Project Background

This project was developed in a Social Research Methods course as a team research proposal. The central idea was to examine whether international conflict, which once felt distant to domestic consumers, now affects household behavior more directly because of real-time news, search engines, social media, and mobile alerts.

The project focuses on U.S.-Iran conflict periods and asks whether South Korean households show stronger precautionary consumption patterns in the current hyper-connected media environment than in earlier periods.

## Research Question

Has the transition into a hyper-connected society changed the intensity of South Korean households' essential-goods consumption response to overseas geopolitical conflict?

## Core Concepts

- **Geopolitical risk**: External political or military conflict that may affect perceived economic stability.
- **Hyper-connected society**: A social environment where conflict information spreads instantly through digital media, search platforms, and real-time notifications.
- **Precautionary consumption**: Household purchasing behavior driven by uncertainty, perceived threat, or future supply concerns.
- **Essential-goods consumption**: Spending on food, daily necessities, medicine, and other basic goods.

## Research Design

The proposal uses a comparative secondary-data research design.

### Time Periods

- Earlier period: U.S.-Iran military tension around the late 1980s to early 1990s
- Current period: U.S.-Iran conflict and tension after 2019, including the Soleimani incident and later regional escalation

### Unit of Analysis

Monthly time-series observations of South Korean household consumption.

### Dependent Variable

Share or growth rate of essential-goods expenditure in household spending.

### Independent Variable

Occurrence of U.S.-Iran conflict or military tension.

### Mediating Variables

- News volume related to U.S.-Iran conflict
- Search interest, where available
- Consumer sentiment index

### Control Variables

- Consumer Price Index
- Exchange rate
- Domestic gasoline price
- Crude oil price or energy-price indicators
- Major economic shocks such as COVID-19 or financial crisis periods

## Data Sources Considered

- KOSIS household expenditure data
- Bank of Korea ECOS indicators
- Korea National Oil Corporation data
- BIGKinds news database
- Naver DataLab search trend data, for recent periods where available

## Methodological Approach

The proposed design combines:

- Difference-in-Differences logic
- Time-series comparison before and after conflict events
- Statistical controls for price and macroeconomic shocks
- Mediation logic using news volume, search interest, and consumer sentiment
- Validity checks for internal validity and construct validity

## Social Science Contribution

The project connects macro-level geopolitical risk with micro-level household behavior. Rather than treating international conflict only as a foreign-policy or macroeconomic issue, the research design asks how conflict information becomes socially meaningful through media exposure and then appears in ordinary consumption behavior.

The project also demonstrates key social research skills:

- Turning an abstract social phenomenon into measurable variables
- Operationalizing concepts such as anxiety, attention, and precautionary behavior
- Designing a feasible secondary-data study
- Identifying confounding variables and validity threats
- Building a defensible research proposal from theory to measurement
